Oyakodon

Ingredients:

  • Chicken thighs - I recommend thighs over breasts because fattier thighs are more flavorful, tender, and forgiving (not like dry, overcooked breasts).
  • Sake - Drizzling a tablespoon of sake over chicken helps remove its gamey odor.
  • Onion
  • Eggs
  • Dashi
  • togarashi
  • Rice
  • Green onions
  1. Prepare the ingredients: Cut the onion, mitsuba/green onions, and chicken and crack the eggs.
  2. Cook the onion in the seasoning mixture. Add the chicken and cook until no longer pink.
  3. Drizzle egg mixture at two separate times. Garnish green onions.
  4. Spoon the finished dish over the bed of steamed rice in a donburi bowl.

    Tips

  • Use Good Japanese Soup Stock (Dashi) What makes this chicken and egg rice bowl authentically Japanese? It is the flavor of dashiâ€”Japanese soup stock. If you are not familiar with dashi, think of this ingredient as the most essential flavor of Japanese cuisine.
  • To achieve an ideal egg texture for delicious oyakodon, aim for egg whites that are somewhat firm but still wobbly and egg yolks that remain soft and runny. Since raw eggs are safe to consume in Japan, oyakodon can look a bit different when it is served in Japan. Do not be shocked if the eggs are a lot more on the runny and raw side there. Add two-thirds of the eggs (ideally more egg whites because they take longer time to cook than egg yolks) to the center of the pan, avoiding the edges where eggs get overcooked. Add the remaining one-third of the eggs (ideally more egg yolks) to the entire surface of the pan.
