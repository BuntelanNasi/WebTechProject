Nasi Goreng

Ingredients:

  • Sambal paste (Make sure you get the one with MSG, tastes better)
  • Day old rice (kept in a fridge)
  • Kecap manis
  • Chicken
  • Shallots
  • Pork fat
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 Wok
  • Vegetable oil
  • Fried shallots
  • Prawn crackers
  1. Heat vegetable oil and swirl around wok to get wok-hay. Then, throw away the oil and put ork fat into wok waiting for it to melt.
  2. Afterwards, put sambal and chicken. Cook until chicken is cooked
  3. After chicken is cooked, add shallots. Cook for 30 seconds and then add day old rice and an egg and mix. After everything is mixed, add kecap manis and mix again.
  4. When everything is mixed, take out of wok and put in a plate
  5. In another pan, fry an egg on high temp to get a crispy exterior and runny yolk
  6. Serve with fried egg on top, fried shallots and if you have prawn crackers

    Tips

  • Day old rice makes it so that the rice don't clump up together. Make sure its at least a day old. Dont refigerate for more than a week!
  • MSG is a controversial ingredient. A lot of people think MSG is very unhealthy and it is in very large amount of consumption. We are only using a little, therefore it should be fine. If you are still scared, substitute a sambal without MSG.
  • Pork fat is very hard to find. When I make nasi goreng, I usually get some of these ingredients from my home country of Indonesia itself, therefore if you can't get pork fat, using vegetable oil itself is fine.
